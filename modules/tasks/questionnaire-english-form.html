Definitions:
Recurrent AFib: A recurrence of AFib after self-termination or after successful treatment with anti-arrhythmic drugs, cardioversion or ablation
Paroxysmal AFib: An episode of AFib that self-terminates or terminates with intervention in ≤7 days
Persistent AFib: A sustained episode of AFib lasting > 7days that does not self-terminate
Permanent AFib: Irreversible AFib despite attempts to restore and/or maintain sinus rhythm
CPAP: Continuous Positive Airway Pressure is a therapeutic device used to treat obstructive sleep apnea. The treatment involves delivering pressurized air to the upper airway via a nasal mask.
